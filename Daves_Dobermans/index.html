Dobermans are Aggressive

This is a very common misconeption among those unfamiliar with the breed. Dobermans have this reputation from a long history of being bred as guard dogs and trained to attack and be particularly protective. They were originally bred in Germany during WW2 to have a dog that would protect, kill and intimidate your enemies, which is one reason the ears were done and the tails were cropped. They looked fierce. This has not been true for a long time, as breeders it's our job to make sure the customer gets what they want and that we breed responsibly. This means that we only take Dobermans that have had a complete health exam, that can pass a behavior test, their lines can be traced back 5 generations at least, and less important have some kind of show or agility awards.

This has been true with breeders for some time now as we do not want to breed undesirable traits, so the end result is a Doberman that is more well behaved than your lab. You can rest assured that your new Doberman will not attack, maim or lash out at you or anyone else for that matter.

Dobermans and Kids

If you have children and are considering purchasing a doberman then you will have no worries about the dogs attitude towards the children. First, our Dobermans are raised around children and second they are very mild mannered and tolerant when it comes to children and actually any human. They get excited to see people, kids and anyone because it means they get to play, which they love to do.

Tails Cropped or Not

We normally crop our Dobermans tails before you even get to see them because it is best to do before they are six months old. However, if you do not want their tail cropped you can request that ahead of time and we will make sure your wishes are met. When we get agility dogs in then we don't crop their tail unless requested because they use their tail as a rudder to keep them quick and agile.

So if you are thinking to crop or not it is a personal decision, it doesn't hurt the dogs in anyway as we do it surgically under anesthesia and it heals very quickly.

Ears Cut and Shaped

This is definitely something that is a personal choice, a lot of veterinarians won't do the procedure anymore. When we first started breeding we had our dobermans ears done and we had to drive quite a ways to find someone willing to do it and I finally understood why. It is really a painful and traumatic event for the dog, and since it does not help in any way whatsoever. So, like my mistake with my very first Doberman I cropped his ears because it was the look that I wanted, and I vowed to never do it again. It is six months of torture for the dog, having to change the bandages every few days and clean the area so it doesn't get infected, it traumatizes the animal and they never forget it.

To show the temperament of Dobermans the whole time we took care of his ears no matter how much it hurt he never once lashed out at us and he still loves us and is loyal today even though we put him through that torture when he was little. So the choice is yours, but we will not do the ears nor do we know anyone in the area that does that procedure.
